I saw this trending topic on [Twitter](https://twitter.com/search?q=%23100DaysOfMLCode&src=tyah) and was momentarily inspired. I am creating this post so that I can catalogue whether I am able to channel this motivation into something good. 

Earlier in the month, I had made an accidental 1 year subscription on Datacamp due to a misunderstanding regarding the verification before paying process on the website. The mistake was mostly mine as I had forgotten that unlike Indian online payment portals, American (maybe everywhere else as well?) do not have a two step authentication process. To compound to that, my card information was already stored because of an earlier transaction. 

Well here I was with a 1 year subscription to the entire site (a very cool feature unlike Coursera or edX where you have to pay for each course separately) and an occasionally burning motivation to be good at this stuff. As a result on Day 1 of this endeavour, I started on the [Machine Learning with  R](https://www.datacamp.com/tracks/machine-learning) skill track on Datacamp. 

I completed two chapters (kNN and Naive Bayes) in the supervised learning for classification course yesterday. There are two chapters left, Logistic Regression and Classification Trees. I hope to complete those two after work today and I will write up a short post detailing what I learnt.

'Til Later
